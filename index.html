About

Technology is only as powerful as the people it lifts.

I am a Computer Science student at Delaware State University with a minor in Finance, operating at the rare crossroads of code, community, and capital. Whether I am writing Python, analyzing data for the Black Male Initiative, or negotiating sponsorship packages, my work is always pointed in the same direction: creating access where it did not exist before.

As an NSF Intern, I work at the frontier of federally supported research and science. On campus, I organized DSU's first ever AI Hackathon, secured $20,000 in sponsorship from Barclays, and built the full logistics framework from the ground up. That event is coming next semester and it is going to be worth showing up for.

Next year I step into the role of Campus Activities Board Business Manager, where I will oversee a $100,000+ budget and curate events that genuinely move the student body. I believe in doing things that matter at scale, and I am just getting started.
